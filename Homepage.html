Idea behind this game

Cricket in India is not just a game. It is the emotion of almost 140 billion people living in the country. It is considered as a sport that unites the whole country. Cricket in India was introduced by the Britishers who ruled India from 1800 till 1947.The Parsees cricket team toured England in 1890s.After 1910s, cricket spread like a forestfire in the Indian subcontinent.Bombay Triangulars began with three teams comprising of Europeans, Parsees and the Hindus. Bombay Gymkhana was established in Bombay(now Mumbai) by the Parsees.The tournament was further extended as Bombay pentangular with teams Hindus,parsees,Muslims,Europeans and the rest comprising of Buddhists,Jews and the Christians.
Eventually, amidst a backdrop of rioting and political unrest across India, the newly formed Board of Control for Cricket in India announced in 1946 that the Pentangular tournament was being abandoned, and being replaced by a zonal competition. The Ranji Trophy, in which regional teams from all over India compete, became the pre-eminent Indian cricket competition.

India won its first ICC Cricket World Cup in 1983 under the captaincy of Kapi Dev. This World cup victory enhanced the growth of Cricket in India.India won its first T20 World Cup in 2007 under MS Dhoni, a young captain from Jharkhand, after the debacle of 2007 ODI World Cup. The advent of IPL boosted T20 Cricket in India and India gained prominence on the World level for successfully hosting a tournament consisting of Indians as well as foreign players as participants of the competition.Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings are the most sucessful teams in IPL winning 5 IPL titles each.The incumbent IPL champions are Kolkata Knight Riders led by Shreyas Iyer.The 8-team competition was expanded in 2010 with two new teams added namely Pune Warriors India and Kochi Tuskers Kerala. These new teams were led by former India captain Sourav Ganguly and former Srilanka captain Mahela Jaywerdene. The Indian Premier League has successfully completed 16 seasons.
In 2015, two teams Chennai Super Kings led by MS Dhoni and Rajasthan Royals led by Ajinkya Rahane were banned for next 2 IPL seasons for the allegation of match fixing. Two new teams Rising Pune Supergiants and Gujarat Lions were incorporated for IPL 2016. These twomteams were led by MS Dhoni(Thala) and Suresh Raina(Chinna Thala). Rising Pune Supergiants were the runner-ups of the competition in 2017 season in which Mumbai Indians won the tournament by one run defeating them. This marked the end of these two teams in IPL.

I have developed this website or game for fun purpose.Indians are passionate about cricket and especially IPL. So i am providing them a platform to implement their business mind and cricketing acumen to build a team of their favourite players.Thank you for visiting this website.
